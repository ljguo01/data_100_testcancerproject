A Closer Look into Respiratory Cancer

Lung Cancer Incidences by Country

In general, there seems to be a trend in which lung cancer crude rates seem to be higher in countries that are more industrialized. For example, more developed countries in North America like USA and Canada have relatively higher crude rates compared to less developed countries in Africa. However, when using GDP per capita as a measurement of industrialization, it seems that there are only moderate correlations with lung cancer crude rates.

GDP per capita has moderate correlation with lung cancer crude rates

  • Luxembourg - 2nd highest GDP, Moderate crude rate of 56
  • Canada - 22nd highest GDP, High crude rate of 81.2
  • China - 72nd highest GDP, High crude rates of 75.1

Investigating Japan and Indonesia

  • Japan - Of men over 20 in Japan, 82.3% of the population were smokers in 1965 which decreased to 19.2% smokers in 2022
  • Indonesia - Cigarette consumption rose from 33 billion sticks in 1970 to 301 billion sticks in 2022.

Notably, these statistic implies that looking at current smoking statistics does not show as strong of a correlation to lung cancer incidences because of the time lag between smoking and lung cancer development. Lung cancer takes around 30-40 years to develop after smoking regularly (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18556244/). This shows that historical smoking rates may be a better predictor of lung cancer incidences, and further examination into the socioeconomic or cultural factors which may have caused the smoking trends would give a more comprehensive understanding and prediction of lung cancer.
