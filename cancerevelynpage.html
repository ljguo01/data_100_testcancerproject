Incidence and Mortality Rates of Different Cancer Types

Incidence and Mortality Rates

Cancers With the Highest Incidence Rates

  1. Gastrointestinal Cancer: 4,905,882 cases
  2. Urogenital Cancer: 3,437,858 cases
  3. Respiratory Cancer: 2,480,675 cases
  4. Breast Cancer: 2,296,840 cases
  5. Thyroid Cancer: 821,214 cases

  1. Gastrointestinal Cancer: 3,324,774 deaths
  2. Respiratory Cancer: 1,817,469 deaths
  3. Urogenital Cancer: 1,128,283 deaths
  4. Breast Cancer: 666,103 deaths
  5. Cervical Cancer: 348,874 deaths

Gastrointestinal Cancers

Gastrointestinal (GI) cancer includes esophageal, stomach, colorectal, liver, gallbladder, and pancreatic cancers. GI cancer has the highest incidence and mortality rates, with 4,905,882 incidences and 3,324,774 deaths in 2022.

GI cancer has a high incidence rate due to its high number of risk factors and common underlying conditions.

Risk Factors

  • Smoking
  • Alcohol Consumption
  • Obesity
  • Dietary Habits

Common Underlying Conditions

  • H. pylori or Hepatitis B/C virus infections in the stomach
  • Cirrhosis in the liver
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease

The increased production of processed foods in recent years has led to increased prevalence of obesity, metabolic disorders, and poor dietary habits. Because these are all risk factors for GI cancers, incidence rates have also increased in recent years.

Reasons for High Mortality Rate

  • Many comorbidities (infection, obesity, etc.)
  • Cases are often advanced when diagnosed
